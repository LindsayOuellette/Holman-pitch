The Problem

A robot can technically work and still fail in practice

The gap between installation success and real-world adoption is where revenue is lost — and where Holman's reputation lives.

⚡ What Holman measures today
  • Robot delivered & turned on
  • Tasks completed (dashboard)
  • Uptime & navigation data
  • Low / high usage triggers
→
⚠ What this misses
  • Workflow fit in the real environment
  • Employee adoption vs. workarounds
  • Expectation misalignment at sale
  • Early warning signs of churn
The Opportunity

Add a structured human layer that bridges technical deployment and long-term adoption — improving customer experience, retention, and the likelihood of second and third robot sales.

Scalability

This builds a capability, not a dependency

📋

Playbooks & Templates

Discovery questions, workflow maps, onboarding scripts, and follow-up guides — documented so any team member can use them without a specialist present.

🔧

Embedded in Existing Processes

Tools and metrics built into Holman's current sales forms, implementation documents, and follow-up routines — not a new layer on top.

🎓

Training & Coaching

Sales, Operations, and Customer Success trained to apply the playbooks. Over time, the role shifts to continuous improvement and complex cases.

Holman doesn't need "40 Lindsays." It needs one Integration & Adoption Lead to build and refine the methods — and a growing team that can apply them through well-designed playbooks, tools, and training.
Business Impact

What Holman gains

🔁

More repeat sales

Structured follow-up and optimization reviews create natural expansion conversations — turning one-robot customers into multi-robot accounts.

🤝

Stronger customer retention

Human-centered deployment catches churn signals early. Customers who see real adoption don't leave.

📐

Better sales conversations

Discovery tools and expectation-setting on the front end mean fewer deployments that land wrong — and fewer painful post-sale surprises.

🧠

Institutional knowledge

Each deployment feeds learning back into playbooks, OEM feedback, and sales narratives — compounding value with every project.

The opportunity: develop a repeatable capability for making robot deployments successful with real people — not just successfully installed.
